Categories and tags help you organize and classify your blog posts, making it easier for readers to discover related content.

## Understanding categories vs tags

**Categories** provide hierarchical organization:
- Broad topic groupings for your content
- Can have parent-child relationships (subcategories)
- Each post typically belongs to one or a few categories
- Appear in category archives and navigation

**Tags** provide flexible labeling:
- Specific keywords or topics
- No hierarchical structure
- Posts can have many tags
- Help readers find content through tag clouds or tag archives

<Info>
  Think of categories as your site's table of contents and tags as the index. Categories organize content into sections, while tags identify specific topics within that content.
</Info>

## Managing categories

### Creating a category

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to categories">
    From the admin dashboard, click on **Categories** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create new category">
    Click the **New Category** button to open the category creation form.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter category details">
    Fill in the required information:
    - **Display Name**: The name shown to readers (e.g., "Technology")
    - **Slug**: URL-friendly identifier (e.g., "technology")
    - **Description**: Brief description of what content belongs in this category
    - **Cover Image**: Optional featured image for the category
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure settings">
    Set additional options:
    - **Priority**: Controls display order (higher numbers appear first)
    - **Template**: Custom template for the category archive page
    - **Post Template**: Default template for posts in this category
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save the category">
    Click **Save** to create the category.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Creating subcategories

To organize categories hierarchically:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create or edit parent category">
    Open the parent category you want to add subcategories to.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create child category">
    Create a new category as described above.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set parent relationship">
    In the category settings, select the parent category from the **Parent Category** dropdown.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** to establish the parent-child relationship.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  When you query posts in a parent category, Halo automatically includes posts from all subcategories unless you enable the "Prevent Parent Post Cascade Query" option in the category settings.
</Note>

### Hiding categories from lists

You can hide categories and their posts from public category lists while still allowing direct access:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Edit the category">
    Open the category you want to hide.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enable hide option">
    Toggle the **Hide from List** option in the category settings.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save changes">
    Click **Save** to apply the setting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  The "Hide from List" option only affects top-level categories. It will also hide all subcategories and their associated posts from category lists on your theme.
</Warning>

### Editing a category

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the category">
    Navigate to **Categories** and locate the category you want to edit.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Open the editor">
    Click on the category name or the **Edit** button.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Make changes">
    Update the name, description, settings, or other properties.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save changes">
    Click **Update** to save your changes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Deleting a category

<Steps>
  <Step title="Remove posts">
    Before deleting, reassign posts to other categories or remove the category assignment from affected posts.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Delete the category">
    Click the **Delete** button next to the category name.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm deletion">
    Confirm the action when prompted. The category will be permanently removed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  If a category has subcategories, you must delete or reassign the subcategories first before deleting the parent category.
</Warning>

## Managing tags

### Creating a tag

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to tags">
    From the admin dashboard, click on **Tags** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create new tag">
    Click the **New Tag** button to open the tag creation form.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter tag details">
    Fill in the required information:
    - **Display Name**: The name shown to readers (e.g., "JavaScript")
    - **Slug**: URL-friendly identifier (e.g., "javascript")
    - **Color**: Optional color code for visual identification (e.g., "#FF5733")
    - **Cover Image**: Optional featured image for the tag archive page
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save the tag">
    Click **Save** to create the tag.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You can also create tags on-the-fly when editing a post. Simply type a new tag name in the tags field, and Halo will create it automatically.
</Tip>

### Editing a tag

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the tag">
    Navigate to **Tags** and locate the tag you want to edit.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Open the editor">
    Click on the tag name or the **Edit** button.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Make changes">
    Update the name, slug, color, cover image, or other properties.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save changes">
    Click **Update** to save your changes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Deleting a tag

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the tag">
    Navigate to **Tags** and find the tag you want to delete.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Delete the tag">
    Click the **Delete** button next to the tag name.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm deletion">
    Confirm the action when prompted. The tag will be removed from all posts and permanently deleted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Deleting a tag only removes the tag itself. Posts that had this tag will remain published and unaffected, except they will no longer be associated with the deleted tag.
</Note>

## Assigning categories and tags to posts

When creating or editing a post:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open post editor">
    Create a new post or edit an existing one.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Access settings tab">
    Click the **Settings** tab in the post editor.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select categories">
    In the **Categories** section, select one or more categories from the dropdown menu.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add tags">
    In the **Tags** section, type tag names or select existing tags. Press Enter after each tag to add it.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save the post">
    Click **Save** or **Update** to apply the changes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Viewing category and tag statistics

The categories and tags pages display useful statistics:

- **Post Count**: Total number of posts (including drafts) associated with the category or tag
- **Visible Post Count**: Number of published and public posts
- **Permalink**: The URL where readers can view all posts in this category or tag

These statistics update automatically as you create, publish, or delete posts.

## Best practices

- **Use categories sparingly**: Create 5-10 main categories for your site. Too many categories can overwhelm readers.
- **Be generous with tags**: Use specific, descriptive tags to help readers find related content.
- **Maintain consistency**: Use standardized naming conventions for categories and tags (e.g., "Web Development" not "web dev" or "webdev").
- **Review regularly**: Periodically audit your categories and tags to merge duplicates or remove unused ones.
- **Plan your hierarchy**: Think about your category structure before creating content. Reorganizing later can be time-consuming.
